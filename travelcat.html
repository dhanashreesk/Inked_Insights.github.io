HISTORICAL PLACES

Qutub Minar : Towering symbol of India's glorious past and architectural marvels

Qutub Minar is a towering minaret located in Delhi, India. It is considered to be one of the finest examples of Indo-Islamic architecture in India and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The minaret stands at a height of 72.5 meters (237.8 feet) and has a base diameter of 14.3 meters (47 feet), tapering to a diameter of 2.7 meters (9 feet) at the top. Continue reading....

Red Fort :Symbol of India's rich cultural heritage

The Red Fort is made of red sandstone and covers an area of about 254.67 acres. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Delhi. The fort has several notable structures, including the Diwan-i-Aam (Hall of Public Audience), Diwan-i-Khas (Hall of Private Audience), Moti Masjid (Pearl Mosque), and the Rang Mahal (Palace of Colours). Rani ki Vav :Mystical world of ancient legends, intricate carvings, and mesmerizing beauty

Rani ki Vav is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered to be one of the most beautiful and well-preserved stepwells in India. The structure is characterized by a series of intricately carved steps that lead down to a large pool of water. The walls of the stepwell are adorned with more than 800 sculptures, which depict various mythological and religious figures, as well as scenes from daily life. Continue reading....

SPIRITUAL PLACES

Shikhar Ji : Pride of Jharkhand

Shikharji, also known as Parasnath Hill, is a sacred pilgrimage site for Jains located in the state of Jharkhand, India. It is believed to be the place where twenty of the twenty-four Tirthankaras, or spiritual leaders of Jainism, attained Moksha, or liberation from the cycle of birth and death. Continue reading....

Golden Temple, Amritsar

The Golden Temple was built in the 16th century by Guru Arjan Dev, the fifth Sikh Guru, and its architecture reflects a unique blend of Hindu and Muslim styles. The temple complex features a large marble-paved courtyard, surrounded by four entrances, and a holy pool known as the Amrit Sarovar. Continue reading....

The Konark Sun Temple is a 13th-century Hindu temple located in the town of Konark in the Indian state of Odisha. It is dedicated to the sun god Surya and is considered to be one of the most significant and impressive examples of temple architecture in India. Continue reading....
